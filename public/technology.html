Biochar Technology & Process

Understanding the science behind permanent carbon removal through advanced pyrolysis technology and biochar production.

Biochar Production Process

Our pyrolysis technology transforms organic biomass waste into stable carbon through a controlled thermal process in the absence of oxygen, creating permanent carbon storage.

1

Biomass Collection

Agricultural waste, forestry residues, and organic materials are collected and prepared for processing.

2

Pyrolysis Processing

High-temperature (400-700°C) thermal decomposition in low-oxygen environment transforms biomass into biochar.

3

Carbon Fixation

Carbon atoms are locked into stable molecular structures, creating permanent storage for 900+ years.

Carbon Removal Science

CO₂ Capture During Growth

Plants naturally capture CO₂ from the atmosphere during photosynthesis, storing carbon in their biomass structures throughout their growth cycle.

Pyrolysis Locks Carbon

High-temperature pyrolysis in oxygen-limited conditions transforms plant carbon into stable aromatic structures that resist decomposition.

900+ Year Permanence

Scientific studies confirm biochar's molecular stability, with carbon remaining sequestered for centuries to millennia in soil and other applications.

Why Biochar Differs from Natural Decomposition

Natural Decomposition

  • • Organic matter decomposes through microbial action
  • • Carbon is released back to atmosphere as CO₂
  • • Process completes within months to years
  • • No permanent carbon storage achieved
  • • Net zero or negative carbon impact

Biochar Production

  • • Controlled pyrolysis prevents normal decomposition
  • • Carbon is fixed in stable aromatic structures
  • • Molecular stability lasts 900+ years
  • • Permanent removal from carbon cycle
  • • Net negative carbon impact achieved
